Why where you live in France shapes your cinema experience

Cinema in France

France is home to one of the world’s richest cinematic traditions. But even in this country, access to cinema is uneven across the territory. Some departments have greater opportunities than others to watch films. But how do the distribution and evolution of cinemas in France influence the population’s access to film culture ?


Access to cinemas in France, an uneven distribution

The number of cinemas on the French territory has been relatively stable since 2003 with a minor decrease followed by a slight increase. There were 2,126 cinemas in 2003 and it declined to 2,019 in 2014 and rose to 2,052 in 2024. Overall, there hasn’t been much change since 2003.

In 2024 the country had 2052 cinemas, yet they were and are still distributed pretty unevenly. In Hautes Alpes, for example, there are 6,129 inhabitants per cinema whereas in Mayotte there are 128,259 inhabitants per cinema. That’s 20 times more.

Some departments in France have way more opportunities to see films. Territoire de Belfort has just 2 cinemas, compared to 83 in Paris. This could be explained by the number of people living there.

But Territoire de Belfort has 69,827 residents per cinema and Paris has 25,700. Paris citizens have way more access to film culture than Territoire de Belfort’s. Inequalities are quite big.

Our data analysis also reveals that the number of cinemas in a department is not linked to the number of residents. It means there can be a department with a large population and very few cinemas, and vice versa.

Calculating all the residents per cinema, we can answer the question “Who really has access to cinemas and film culture”. Hautes-Alpes, Rhône and Savoie are the most well distributed areas, and they all have more than 20 cinemas. Most of the departments with fewer than 10 cinemas face really limited access.

This shows how the accessibility to film culture is really impacted by the geographical factors.


Cultural diversity and the importance of Art House cinemas

The distribution and accessibility is not the only factor of the unevenness of the film culture in France. It is great to have many cinemas, but what if they all screen the same films ? The culture is then restricted by diversity not quantity.

In France there is a type of cinema called "Art et Essai" also known as "Art House" cinemas. They usually show films made with lower budget or that are less popular. This type of cinema has become more successful in recent years.

In fact, there were only 969 Art House cinemas in 2003 then 1,444 in 2014 and finally 1,299 in 2024. In 2024, Art House cinemas represented 63% of cinemas in France : it shows the will of offering a wider selection of films to the audience.

People now have better access to films that are not as commercial as the big ones we usually see. Cultural diversity has increased with more than half of the cinemas being Art House.


Evolution of the cinema

As we’ve mentioned before, the total number of cinemas has remained quite stable since 2003. But the number of screens in the cinemas has increased.

Since 2003, there are more and more cinemas with more than 10 screens. In 2003 there were only 74 cinemas in that case but it rose to 106 in 2014 and then to 128 in 2024.

In the span of 21 years, the number of cinemas with 5 screens or less has steadily decreased from 1,905 to 1,697. And the cinemas that have in between 6 and 10 screens have also been slowly increasing.

It shows how cinemas tend to get bigger now than before.



Evolution also comes with modernization. In 2009 there were only a few cinemas that could show 3D films. Since 3D became a trend in the early 2010s, this number rose rapidly from 98 to 1086 and then increased steadily to reach 1,140 in 2024.

The evolution and modernization of the cinemas has made them more attractive and also capable of serving larger audiences with more screens which increase its accessibility.



So, the access to film culture depends as much on where you live and what kind of cinema is around. But it has been getting better in recent years with way more Art House cinemas and modernization.
